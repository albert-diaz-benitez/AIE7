Low-income students often face significant barriers when it comes to affording higher education. It's important to know that various financial aid options are available to help alleviate some of these pressures. For instance, federally subsidized student loans can be a lifeline for those who demonstrate financial need. With these loans, the government covers the interest while the student is enrolled at least half-time in college and during certain periods, which can significantly lessen the financial burden on low-income borrowers.

Filing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is a vital step in accessing federal aid programs, including loans and grants designed specifically for low-income individuals. Completing the FAFSA not only opens doors to loan opportunities but also enables students to apply for Pell Grants, which are awarded based on exceptional financial need and do not require repayment.

In addition to federal aid, there are numerous private organizations and state-sponsored programs that offer loan options tailored to support low-income students. It is crucial to explore and utilize federal aid options fully before considering private loans, as federal loans typically provide more favorable terms, including lower interest rates and more flexible repayment plans. For instance, current federal loan repayment options include Income-Based Repayment (IBR), which can be particularly beneficial for borrowers who are experiencing financial difficulties.
